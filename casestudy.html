Birmingham Community Foundation Web App Redesign

Overview

The community website redesign project aims to modernize and improve the existing website to create a more user-friendly experience for the community. The new website will be designed to serve as a comprehensive source of information for local residents, businesses, visitors and volunteers.

Insight

Users are unable to navigate swiftly through pages due to the clutter of information and images and UI patterns that are not accessible therefore causing confusion and frustration.

Problem

Adults who are new to a community need to feel more confident that they can access content like training, education, local events and activities because this will help them get more involved in their communities.

solution

Simplify the navigation and user flow to enable users to access comprehensive source of information about community activities, educational programmes and more.

RESEARCH GOALS

To better understand the user needs for easy user-friendly experience and navigation for the community, i and my team had to conduct a user research to help us understand usability pain points of the website and also usability problems for the audience. In order to achieve this we conducted an interview and survey questions for users and stakeholders of the community foundation website

USER PERSONA

After the interview was conducted, User Persona was created which gave a clear insight of feedbacks from users and understanding their pain points while using the current community foundation website.
    PAIN POINTS
  • Since she doesn't own a desktop system, she attempts to access the website using her mobile browser but soon realizes that the website is not compatible with mobile devices.
  • The website is a bit cluttered, and it is not easy to find the required information.
  • The font sizes and background colours are not suitable, making it difficult for her to read the context.

FEATURE PRIOTIZATION

Moving forward i and my team conducted the feature priotization matrix based on competitor audit and the I Like, I Wish and What if method. This helped us in understanding the key features that will be required in the website redesign.

STORY BOARD

I created the story board from our user scenario which is “Annie who enjoys to meet people and learn new skill whenever she's in a new location”. We created a storyboard which was further used to create our user flow, and also lead to site mapping.

LOW FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

My teammates and I embarked on designing a responsive web interface. During the design phase, our team split into two groups: one focused on mobile design and the other on desktop design. I was part of the web design team, responsible for creating low fidelity to high fidelity designs. However, despite working separately, we reconvened as a team to analyze both designs, provide feedback, and iterate on our designs collectively.

USABILITY TEST

During the usability test, our team members actively participated in evaluating our low fidelity prototype. This process provided valuable insights and solutions that guided us in making improvements before moving forward with the high fidelity prototype.

FINAL UI DESIGN
